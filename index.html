Monday — June 22: Fly to Japan (travel day only)Long flight + jet-lag management. Keep it simple and rest-focused.

Plan

  • Fly + jet lag management (no sightseeing planned in this itinerary since you arrive in Japan on Tuesday).
  • Bring kid “survival kits”: snacks, wipes, small toys/books, and a spare outfit.
  • Pre-plan tomorrow: set out a simple outfit + one quick “if energy allows” option for Tuesday evening.
Tuesday — June 23: Arrive NRT 14:55 → Hotel (Maihama) + easy eveningArrivals + check-in, then a gentle first-night Tokyo stretch and dinner.

Arrival timing

  • Arrive: Narita (NRT) at 14:55 (Tuesday, June 23).
  • Typical NRT → hotel door-to-door: ~75–120 min (longer in rush hour or with multiple transfers).
  • Plan for: immigration + bags + kids + restroom breaks = ~45–90 min at the airport.

Getting to the hotel (family-friendly options)

  • Option A (easiest with kids): Airport Limousine Bus
    • Take a Limousine Bus from NRT to the Tokyo Disney Resort / Maihama area.
    • Then short taxi/shuttle/walk to Grand Nikko Tokyo Bay Maihama (depending on drop-off).
    • Time: typically ~60–90 min on the bus + last-mile.
  • Option B (reliable rail): Narita Express + JR
    • Narita Express (N'EX) → Tokyo Station
    • JR Keiyo/Musashino Line → Maihama Station
    • Taxi/local shuttle to the hotel.
    • Time: typically ~75–110 min + transfers/waits.

Optional stretch (Skytree + Asakusa, only if you have energy)

  • Time check: with an NRT landing at 14:55, aim to be checked into the hotel roughly by 17:30–18:30. Only do this if you can start the outing by ~18:30 at the latest.
  • Short Skytree Town loop (aim 60–90 min total).
  • If you want one indoor stop: Sumida Aquarium (~45–90 min).
  • Quick Nakamise Street snack stroll in Asakusa (~30–45 min), then head back early.
  • If kids get tired, skip this and stay in the hotel/Ikspiari area.

Easy evening ideas (low-effort)

  • Dinner + stretch: Ikspiari (~1–2 hours) only if you’re checked in and everyone has energy (best start ~18:00).
  • Prep for the next morning: set out clothes, charge batteries, pack a small day bag.

Family of 6 tips

  • On arrival day, prioritize food + showers + early sleep over sightseeing.
  • Have one adult handle tickets/transport while another focuses on kids/bathrooms.
  • Keep one small “airport bag” accessible: wipes, snacks, spare clothes, chargers.

Hotel/area tips

  • If you use rail, aim to reach Maihama before dinner time to avoid late-night transfers.
  • Consider luggage forwarding for easier station navigation with kids.

  • Hydration + shade: this is a good day to aim for more indoor time in the afternoon.

Disneyland priorities (family-friendly rotation)

  • Older-kid headliners: Big Thunder Mountain, Indiana Jones Adventure, Pirates of the Caribbean.
  • Show + break strategy: pick 1 parade/show and treat it as your daily reset.

Parent rotation suggestion

  • 2 adults rotate between big rides while another handles snacks/stroller and a backup indoor option.
  • Use the toddler's rest window for the longest queue attraction first.

Family of 6 tips

  • Stroller + baby basics: pack wipes and a spare outfit for the quick messy moments.
  • Meal timing: aim for a sit-down meal slightly before the main lunch rush to avoid long lines with a 1-year-old.
  • Hydration & shade: take micro-breaks every ~90 minutes.

Disney Premier Access & ride scheduling

  • Paid “fast lane” here is Disney Premier Access: buy timed entry or shorter-wait access for select rides and some parades/shows through the Tokyo Disney Resort app (fees and eligible experiences change—check the official page and in-app list on your park day).
  • Before the trip: install the app, create/link My Disney accounts for anyone who might purchase Premier Access, and confirm everyone’s park tickets are tied to profiles in the app so one adult isn’t stuck doing everything at gate time.
  • Family of 6 economics: Premier Access is usually per guest, per experience—costs add up fast. Pick one or two true “must-dos” that tend to spike (often the newest or flashiest rides) rather than buying every offering.
  • Pacing: after entering, pick return windows that fit meals, parade/show times, and stroller naps. Assign one parent as “app captain” to grab windows so the rest of the group isn’t scanning phones at every turnstile.
  • Standby strategy: rope-drop one compact land cluster, do 1–2 short-queue rides while lines are thin, then either standby your single top priority early or switch to Premier Access if waits are exploding—avoid zig-zagging across the park for marginal savings.
  • On crowded days the app may also use Standby Pass / entry rules for certain experiences—treat the app’s prompts as the authority, not third-party blogs.

Venue tips (Disneyland)

  • If crowds are heavy, do a "land loop": go where you can move fastest, then lock in your show/parade time.

DisneySea priorities

  • Journey to the Center of the Earth (~20–45 min incl. waits)
  • Nemo & Friends SeaRider (~20–40 min incl. waits)
  • Toy Story Mania (~15–35 min incl. waits)
  • Mermaid Lagoon indoor play (~45–75 min) — excellent for rain/cooler breaks

Parent rotation suggestion

  • Indiana Jones (~20–45 min incl. waits)
  • Tower of Terror (~20–45 min incl. waits)

Family of 6 tips

  • Pick one indoor anchor (Mermaid Lagoon) for naps, feeding, and rain breaks.
  • Use a two-adult rotation: 2 adults do a big ride while others do a kid zone, then swap.
  • Keep a mobile battery per parent for maps, tickets, and photos.

Disney Premier Access & ride scheduling

  • Same system as Disneyland: Disney Premier Access is the paid, app-based option for select rides and some entertainment (offerings and prices vary—check the app after you enter).
  • Sea-specific pacing: headliners like Journey to the Center of the Earth, Toy Story Mania!, and newer Fantasy Springs rides can dominate waits—decide as a family whether you’ll rope-drop one, standby once, or fund one Premier Access block for the worst offender, then fill the day with lower-stress experiences.
  • Stack windows carefully: don’t book Premier Access return times back-to-back across opposite ends of the park with small kids; pair a return window with a nearby meal, show, or indoor break (Mermaid Lagoon, shops, cafés).
  • Rotation + Premier Access: if only some riders meet height rules, confirm how many passes you need before purchasing—wasted purchases are expensive at ×6 people.
  • If the app offers Standby Pass or other entry controls for an experience you want, grab them early; capacity can disappear on peak summer days.

Venue tips (DisneySea)

  • Prioritize the one attraction with the longest typical waits early, then fill with shorter queues.
  • Rain plan: Mermaid Lagoon + indoor shows first; save outdoor areas for gaps in weather.
